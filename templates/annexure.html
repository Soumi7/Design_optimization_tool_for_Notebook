Pogo load §  Load encountered by Notebook enclosure during normal day-to-day usage (being picked up, moved, packed into a travel bag, palm rest, typing, etc.). §  The application of pogo load causes the failure of laptop components. For example, deterioration of fan performance, abnormal fan noise, a permanent deformation in the fan grill area, battery failure, internal shorting, etc. §  To prevent these failures, the chassis needs to be designed with sufficient stiffness and integrity to withstand Pogo loads, making it an important criterion in chassis design. Critical area of concern- Pogo loading "§  Highlighted green region on D cover is stiff because of geometry and bonding of C and D cover can withstand higher Pogo load before failure. " § Red region typically in fan grill area (fan location ) considered as critical failure area for Pogo loading because it is unsupported and chassis deflection is higher in this region. Thermal solution (Die area) is not critical because die pressure limit is up to 800 psi can withstand higher pogo loads. Types of fan orientation in Notebook design Critical area for pogo loading Two types of fan orientation used in Notebook design : 1) Fan hub facing D cover 2) Fan hub facing C cover Fan hub facing D cover Fan hub facing C cover "§  This is conventional fan orientation provides better thermal performance but less load carrying capacity when compared to hub facing C cover." § This fan orientation affects thermal performance of Notebook but significantly increase Pogo load carrying capacity. Recommended to use this fan orientation if there is no significant reduction in thermal performance. Failure Mode: D cover touching fan hub Failure Modes: Fan blade touching fan casing Fan hub touching keyboard Hub failure Blade failure Hub failure § D cover deflects and touches to fan hub due to theapplication of pogo load, causes abnormal fan noise and change in fan performance. §  Due to the application of pogo load, first D cover deflects and touches the fan casing, after fan casing (Motor side) will carry forward the load and deflects the fan causes either blade failure or hub failure. § Load required to stop the fan depends on only stiffness of D cover. §  Load required to stop the fan depends on combined stiffness of D cover and fan casing. "§  To avoid fan blade failure and increase Pogo load carrying capacity, the internal air gap between fan blade and fan casing ( hub side) should be greater than air gap available between hub and keyboard backplate." Fan grill patterns Gasket / Emboss "§  The touchpoints are provided on fan casing to restrict the deflection of D cover and improve the Pogo load carrying capacity of the notebook." §  Embosses are the projections on the fan casing made of same material as casing (Mostly steel and copper). "§  Gaskets are typically made of Neoprene, polyurethane rubber. The desirable property of the gasket is that it will initially compress up to a certain thickness at less pressure and then it will a reach solid state (It will not deform on further application of high pressure)." "§  Emboss provides higher Pogo load carrying capacity compared to gasket because of its high stiffness and location near to circular cutout on fan casing. " Note: Certain factors were considered while developing this tool 1) Location and number of gasket/Emboss on fan casing are fixed. 2) 0.35 mm tolerance considered for embossing. If air gap is less than 0.35 mm, emboss feature will not work. 3) No tolerance considered for gasket. 4) For PC ABS material, Emboss height is restircted up to 0.6 mm. 5) Embossing is not considered for fan orientation-hub facing C cover. All predictedresults are with respect to gaskets only.
